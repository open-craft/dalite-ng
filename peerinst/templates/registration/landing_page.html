{% trans "Features" %}

{% blocktrans %} myDALITE is a free open-source web-based platform that promotes deep learning. Its pedagogical foundations are guided by social learning and based on learning principles and practical know-how. It engages students in cognitive tasks that include written self-explanation, comparison and evaluation of explanations produced by peers, and reflection. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Peer Instruction: In-Class, At-Home, or in MOOCs" %}

{% blocktrans %} Peer instructionis an active learning strategy popularized by Eric Mazur from Harvard University. It is a strategy that involves a sequence of steps that promote deep thinking and discussion around the selection of the answers for multiple choice conceptual questions â€“ i.e. think, vote, discuss and reflect, revote. Teachers can use the same questions in-class in polling mode, or assign those items to students for out-of-class work through their learning management system (LMS). Instructional designers can access the same server for MOOCs for thousands of students. Teachers can peruse content based on analytics, and get reports for how their students are doing over time. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Most active disciplines" %}

{% blocktrans %} myDALITE.org is a free, hosted service, where teachers can reuse the growing database of questions spanning subjects in science and the humanities. Funded by the SALTISE/S4 grant, the project aims to be the home of an open source community of quality, higher-order learning resources, built for Teachers, by Teachers. If you don't find the type of content you are looking for, you can make new question items of your own and contribute to the community. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "History" %}

{% blocktrans %} The Distributed Active Learning and Interactive Technology Environment (myDALITE) is the product of a design-based experiment that consisted of five iterative phases carried out over a nine-year period:

  • - the initial co-design development, where teachers worked with researchers to design the tool
  • - a research phase, wherein several studies showed thebenefits and power of extending Peer Instruction in this way
  • - a reformulation as an LTI-complaint tool for use in Learning Management Systems, with successful integrations now in OpenEdx, Moodle, and eConcordia, which brought the research idea to a technical maturity ready for scale
  • - the preparation for use by practitioners, such that the tool now has many of the UX/UI components teachers expect of a modern, stable web based product: one that can count on to help regularly engage their students with higher order learning tasks
    • {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "The S4 project" %}

{% blocktrans %} The SALTISE/S4 project is a major 5-year multi-institution infrastructure grant hosted at Dawson College. The madate is to develop a data-driven platform founded on three pillars:

  • - Teachers: a place where where the pedagogical strategies and artefacts teachers produce can be hosted and showcased to a network of colleagues, and that can be iteratively improved based on the data associated with the different contexts there are used in
  • - Researchers: a platform that enables learning science researchers to engage directly with practitionners in a seamless way, collaborating to ask new questions about effective pedagogical practices and learning proceesses, and ultimately bring their expertise to the field faster
  • - Students: putting students in the research-design loop as we build novel learning resources, while providing them with a persistent place to monitor how their knowldge evolves not just over one semester, but across the years. {% endblocktrans %}
